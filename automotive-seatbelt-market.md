<p><p>The automotive seatbelt market features various types designed for passenger safety. ELR (Emergency Locking Retractor) three-point seatbelts automatically adjust to the occupant's movement, locking during sudden stops or collisions. Conversely, ALR (Automatic Locking Retractor) three-point seatbelts lock into place once buckled, providing a secure fit that prevents excess slack. Both types enhance occupant protection by minimizing movement during accidents, while their distinct mechanisms cater to different safety scenarios and user preferences, contributing to overall vehicle safety systems.</p></p>
