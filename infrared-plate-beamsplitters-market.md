<p><p>Infrared plate beamsplitters are essential components in optical systems, separating light into different paths. The market includes various materials: </p><p>1. Calcium fluoride (CaF2) offers excellent thermal stability and low absorption in the infrared range, ideal for high-performance applications.  </p><p>2. Potassium bromide (KBr) is favored for its good transmission characteristics but is hygroscopic, limiting its use in humid environments.  </p><p>3. Zinc selenide (ZnSe) combines high durability with effective infrared transmission, suitable for laser-related applications.  </p><p>4. Other materials may include germanium and silicon, each providing unique properties for diverse applications.</p></p>
