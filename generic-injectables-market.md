<p><p>The generic injectables market is primarily divided into two types: small molecules and large molecules. Small molecule injectables consist of low molecular weight compounds, typically synthesized through chemical processes, making them relatively simpler and less expensive to manufacture. In contrast, large molecule injectables are derived from biological sources and include biologics, such as monoclonal antibodies and proteins. These products usually involve complex manufacturing processes and higher development costs, catering to specialized treatments in areas like oncology and autoimmune diseases.</p></p>
